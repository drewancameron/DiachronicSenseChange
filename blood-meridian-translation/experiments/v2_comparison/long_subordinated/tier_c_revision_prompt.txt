## Style and Sense Issues (from review)
  - ὡς Ἰνδὸς σκευάζεσθαι: UNIDIOMATIC GREEK: This is a calque from English 'dressing himself as an Indian'. Greek would more naturally express this as assuming the appearance or guise of an Indian
    → Suggested: Ἰνδοῦ σχῆμα λαμβάνειν or Ἰνδῷ ὁμοιοῦσθαι
  - σταθμὸν λαμβάνειν: WRONG SENSE: σταθμός primarily means 'quarters' or 'station' in a military sense, not taking up position for ambush or begging
    → Suggested: χώραν καταλαμβάνειν or ἵστασθαι
  - εἴ τι ἀργύριον δοῖεν: UNIDIOMATIC GREEK: This construction is too direct a calque from English 'if they would give him money'. Greek would use a more natural expression for asking for money
    → Suggested: εἰ βούλοιντο ἀργύριόν τι διδόναι or εἰ ἀργυρίου μεταδοῖεν

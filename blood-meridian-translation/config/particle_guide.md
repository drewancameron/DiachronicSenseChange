# Particle Style Guide for Blood Meridian Translation

## Core principle

McCarthy's prose is mostly **asyndetic** (no connective at all) or uses bare **"and"**. The Greek should follow this: prefer asyndeton or καί. Use μέν...δέ and other particles only when the English signals a genuine logical relationship.

## When to use what

### Asyndeton (no particle)
Use when McCarthy uses a full stop or comma-splice between independent clauses with no conjunction:
- "He is pale and thin. He stokes the fire." → ὠχρός ἐστι καὶ ἰσχνός. σκαλεύει τὸ πῦρ.
- No δέ, no γάρ, no οὖν — just the next sentence.

### καί
Use when McCarthy writes "and" — his most common conjunction. Also for his cascading "and...and...and" chains:
- "he walks in the streets and hears tongues" → περιπατεῖ ἐν ταῖς ὁδοῖς καὶ ἀκούει γλώσσας

### τε...καί / τε
Use for things that belong together as a natural pair or list, where McCarthy coordinates without contrast:
- "the father never speaks her name, the child does not know it" → ὁ πατὴρ οὐδέποτε λέγει τὸ ὄνομα αὐτῆς, τὸ τε παιδίον οὐ γινώσκει αὐτό
- τε signals "and furthermore / and also" — additive, not adversative

### δέ
Use ONLY when there is a genuine shift, contrast, or change of subject that the English signals:
- "He fled. **But** he leaned against the bar" → ὁ μὲν ἔφυγεν, ὁ δὲ προσεκλίθη τῷ ξύλῳ
- New scene or subject change: "The reverend had stopped. **[Now]** the tall man pushed forward" → ὁ ἱερεὺς ἐπαύσατο. ὁ δὲ ὑψηλὸς προῆλθεν

### μέν...δέ
Use for explicit contrasts between two things:
- "He is not big **but** he has big wrists" → οὐ μέγας μέν ἐστιν, μεγάλους δὲ ἔχει τοὺς καρπούς
- Must be a genuine antithesis, not just two consecutive facts

### γάρ
Use when McCarthy gives an explanation or reason (rare in his style):
- "now he put it on again" (explaining why he'd removed the hat) → νῦν γὰρ πάλιν ἐπέθηκεν αὐτόν

### οὖν
Almost never. McCarthy doesn't do "therefore." Reserve for the judge's formal rhetoric only.

## Common LLM errors to avoid

1. **δέ as default connective** — the LLM treats δέ like English "and." It isn't. δέ implies contrast or topic shift. Most of McCarthy's sentences should be asyndetic or use καί.

2. **Over-using μέν...δέ** — this is a balanced antithesis structure. McCarthy rarely balances. Don't impose symmetry.

3. **γάρ where none is needed** — don't explain what McCarthy leaves unexplained.

4. **Missing asyndeton** — McCarthy's power comes from sentences placed next to each other with NO connective. If the English has a period, the Greek should usually have a period. Don't bridge with particles.
